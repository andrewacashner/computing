Seneca music

  • Vocal with support from water drum, rattle (horn, or in ceremony, turtle shell)
  • Almost always singing and dance together
  • Ceremonial vs. Social
    • Privileged/protected knowledge, closed to non-Senecas and restricted within the Seneca community
    • Annual longhouse ceremonies and medicine-society rituals: The power of “sending a song”

Social songs

  • Yöëdza’ge:ka:’ gaëno’shö’, “Earth Songs”
  • Variety of occasions and functions, including community “Sing” (Easter and Thanksgiving, competitive)
  • Standing Quiver and Old Moccasin Dance, common opening
  • Oral tradition learned with strict protocols
  • Ehsga:nye’: “New Women’s Dance” – genre where innovation is allowed/required
