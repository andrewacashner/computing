Profile

Andrew Cashner is a musician and scholar of music, specializing in devotional music of early modern Mexico and Spain. He is a lecturer and PhD candidate at the University of Chicago, supported by a Dissertation Completion Fellowship from the American Council of Learned Societies and the Mellon Foundation.

The value of his scholarship has been recognized by a Jacob K. Javits Fellowship from the US Department of Education (2010—2014), and by grants from the American Musicological Society and the Council for European Studies at Columbia University. His research and teaching emphasize experiencing music in community as a gateway to historical and cross-cultural understanding. Information for students on current and past courses may be found on the teaching page.

Andrew’s dissertation, Faith, Hearing, and the Power of Music in Hispanic Villancicos, 1600—1700, interprets Hispanic villancicos as sources for historical theological understandings of music’s power. A new article, Playing Cards at the Eucharistic Table, has been published in the Journal of Early Modern History, vol. 18, no. 4 (2014), and is available here. More information is available on the research page and at Academia.edu.

Andrew performs regularly on piano, organ, and harpsichord. He is organist and pianist at St. Peter's Episcopal Church in Chicago, and directs the Gregorian-chant ensemble Quilisma at the University of Chicago. His compositions include choral music, songs, and solo piano pieces. Audio and video recordings may be found on the performance page.

He earned a Bachelor of Music degree in piano performance from Lawrence University and a Master of Sacred Music degree with emphasis in organ performance and theology from the University of Notre Dame, and also studied piano performance at the New England Conservatory of Music.
