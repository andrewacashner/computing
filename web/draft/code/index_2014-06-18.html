Profile

Andrew Cashner is a musician and scholar of music, specializing in devotional music of early modern Mexico and Spain. He is a lecturer and PhD candidate at the University of Chicago. His research and teaching emphasize experiencing music in community as a gateway to historical and cross-cultural understanding.

A former Jacob K. Javits Fellow, he is now completing his dissertation, “Faith, Hearing, and the Power of Music in Hispanic Villancicos, 1600—1700,” with the support of a fellowship from the American Council of Learned Societies. The value of his research has also been recognized by grants from the American Musicological Society, the Council for European Studies at Columbia University, and the Center for Latin American Studies at the University of Chicago.

A new article, “Playing Cards at the Eucharistic Table,” has been published in the Journal of Early Modern History, vol. 18, no. 4 (2014), and is available here.

He performs regularly on piano, organ, and harpsichord. His compositions include choral music, songs, and solo piano pieces.
